Alzheimer's Disease is estimated to affect over 55 million people worldwide, a number expected to grow as populations age. The disease causes progressive loss of memory and cognitive function, driven by the accumulation of amyloid plaques and tau tangles in the brain.

Research is advancing on multiple fronts: immunotherapy to clear damaging proteins, neuroprotective drugs, and approaches that target the earliest stages of the disease before symptoms appear. Clinical trials are essential to moving these discoveries from the lab to people who need them.
