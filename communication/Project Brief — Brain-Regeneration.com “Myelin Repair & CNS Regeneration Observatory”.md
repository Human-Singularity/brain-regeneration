# Project Brief — Brain-Regeneration.com “Myelin Repair & CNS Regeneration Observatory” {#project-brief-—-brain-regeneration.com-“myelin-repair-&-cns-regeneration-observatory”}

## Summary {#summary}

[**Gregory MS**](https://Gregory-MS.com?utm_source=project-brief) is our current project that gathers all the relevant research in the field of Multiple Sclerosis. Our goal is to expand it and **help speed up research into Brain Regeneration.**

Gregory-MS is a 100% donor-funded, and uses an open-source AI platform created in 2019 after founder Bruno Amaral’s multiple sclerosis (MS) diagnosis. Gregory continuously harvests and de-duplicates scientific literature and clinical-trial records, applies ML models to rank relevance, and pushes tailored alerts to researchers and patients within hours of publication. The live MS Observatory already tracks 48 drugs/therapies and a dedicated stem-cell channel. [https://gregory-ms.com/observatory/](https://gregory-ms.com/observatory/?utm_source=project-brief)

The next phase is to build a **knowledge hub** that:

- Provides real-time tracking of published research and clinical trials;  
- maps every therapeutic avenue able to reverse damage caused by MS, Alzheimer’s Disease, Parkinson’s and other neurodegenerative diseases (e.g., remyelination, neuroinflammation control, stem-cell approaches);  
- lists and monitors associated clinical trials for these conditions;  
- continues Gregory’s real-time alerting for investigators;  
- provides patient-centric views highlighting practical impact.

We are looking for strategic partners and senior scientific advisors to steer scope, validate curation criteria, and amplify reach through their networks.

## 1\. Background {#1.-background}

This project aims to be an improved version of [Gregory-MS.com](http://Gregory-MS.com), a website that uses AI to filter the most relevant research around Multiple Sclerosis (MS). The MS version began in 2021 and is an independent endeavour, funded by donations from patients, doctors, and researchers.

Behind the scenes, the MS project uses an open-source software called GregoryAi which was developed to find, aggregate, and categorise the latest scientific research, using Machine Learning models trained to identify papers with positive patient outcomes and novel treatments.

The brain-regeneration project aims to widen the first Gregory-MS project to all areas where brain repair is the main goal, while at the same time improving the quality of information and the user experience for everyone.

### Champions {#champions}

A Champion is a person or organisation that works in the field of scientific or academic research and who has the expertise to guide the creation of an area of the website dedicated to their field.

Champions can involve themselves at different levels of commitment.

1. **Provide a list of searches** that will populate their area of the website with relevant papers. These searches can use any number of journals, archives and other indexers. For example, PubMed, MedRxiv, BioMedCentral, and many others.  
2. **Provide a list of categories** to group the papers within their subject of research.  
3. **Help build legitimacy** for the project by making their contribution and support public.  
4. Disseminate the project to their network (Optional).  
5. Assist in training the Machine Learning models (Optional). In practice, this involves flagging the recent research as relevant or not-relevant for the brain-regeneration goal. It requires a slightly bigger investment in time and therefore we do not ask our champions to include as part of their participation.

Champions are also encouraged to provide feedback on the new website and the GregoryAi features, so that their requests can be added to the roadmap of the Open-Source software.

#### Current Champions {#current-champions}

##### REGENERAR {#regenerar}

The [REGENERAR](https://REGENERAR.eu) research project "Improving the Effectiveness and Safety of Epigenetic Editing in Brain Regeneration", led by researcher Lino Ferreira from the Faculty of Medicine of the University of Coimbra, the Center for Neuroscience and Cell Biology of the University of Coimbra (CNC-UC), and the associated laboratory Center for Innovative Biomedicine and Biotechnology (CIBB), aims to use epigenetic tools that allow certain brain cells to be reprogrammed into neurons. Essentially, it involves using what the human body already has, manipulating cells that have increased in number after the disease process into neuronal cells that are very important for brain function.

### Supporters

Supporters are organisations or institutions that contribute to the project with resources, services, or institutional backing. Their contribution may take the form of funding, infrastructure, access to their membership or community, or institutional endorsement that strengthens the project's credibility.

Supporters are not expected to produce content or guide scientific scope. Their involvement is:

* Provide resources or services that sustain the project's operations.  
* Lend institutional credibility by making their support public.  
* Facilitate access to their member communities for outreach (optional).

### Ambassadors {#ambassadors}

The science community includes several stakeholders who are not doctors or researchers; they can be patients, patient advocates, podcasters, bloggers, and other kinds of content creators.

These stakeholders are an important bridge to reach a wider group of interested and to gather their feedback to help us improve the project.

Ambassadors can also choose their level of involvement.

1. **Disseminate the project to their network**. We do not have a dedicated communication team, and therefore depend on ambassadors to reach relevant audiences.  
2. **Help us share important milestones and new features**.  
3. Provide feedback on the website and development of new areas.

## 4\. Partnership model {#4.-partnership-model}

The options for partnership with the project are still under debate and depend on the availability of partners and the possibility of obtaining funding.

- **Role flexibility:** from light-touch scientific mentorship to deeper co-development.  
- **In-kind contributions welcomed:** advisory time, domain expertise, data access, and dissemination.

## Questions and Answers {#questions-and-answers}

1. **Platform & data**

   - What are the primary data sources, and how often are they refreshed?

     - The MS project has an up-to-date list of sources and query details available at [https://gregory-ms.com/sources/](https://gregory-ms.com/sources/). For this project, we will use the same websites with different search queries.  
   - How is the AI relevance score validated? Are false-positive rates published?

     - We do not have any team member with knowledge to validate our training of the Machine Learning models. The current data set was annotated by Bruno Amaral, and the algorithms that are in place were developed by [Nova SBE](https://www.novasbe.unl.pt/en/)  
   - Can we export data into our own BI tools or EHR systems?

     - Yes, the API is open, and other means to export the data can be arranged.  
   - What security and GDPR safeguards are in place?

     - We store as little data as possible from the subscribers, and use a privacy-friendly analytics system that does not use cookies or store any Personal Identifiable Information (PII)  
     - Our analytics can be viewed and audited at this link: [https://analytics.gregory-ms.com/share/bEuESUpF/GregoryMS](https://analytics.gregory-ms.com/share/bEuESUpF/GregoryMS)

   

2. **Governance & IP**

   - Under which open-source licence is the code released?

     - Creative Commons — Attribution 4.0 International — CC BY 4.0  
     - The code is available on GitHub [https://github.com/Human-Singularity/brain-regeneration](https://github.com/Human-Singularity/brain-regeneration)  
   - Who retains ownership of the derivative analytics we create?

     - We aim to be an open-science project and therefore that data should be freely available to all stakeholders.  
   - How is long-term funding secured beyond donations?

     - This needs to be defined.

   - What happens if the project is spun off into a separate domain?

     - The purpose of securing the domain brain-regeneration.com was to make the project as broad as possible and avoid the need to spin off in a new direction. If that need does come up, it will have to be discussed with all the partners.

   

3. **Engagement & expectations**

   - What is the minimum advisory time commitment?

     - We have not set any minimum commitment, and therefore have outlined options for involvement on section 3, Project Roles.   
   - Are there opportunities for joint grant applications?

     - Yes, but we have not pursued this avenue due to a lack of resources.  
   - Is there liability insurance for medical-decision support tools?

     - No, our role is to gather and curate information. The project is not meant as a diagnosis tool.  
   - Will partner logos or endorsements be public?

     - Yes, we aim for total transparency.  
4. **Impact measurement**

   - Which KPIs will define success for patients vs. researchers?

     - Overall KPIs

       - Visitors to the website  
       - Subscribers in the different stakeholder groups (Patients, Doctors, Researchers)  
     - Patient KPIs

       - Subscribers who identify as patients, and optionally, their area of focus  
       - Quantitative and qualitative feedback from an annual survey  
     - Researcher KPIs

       - Subscribers who identify as researchers  
   - How will user feedback be captured and acted upon?

     - Annual Survey  
     - Contact form on website  
     - Public bug tracking via GitHub issues  
     - Privileged communication channel with key partners
