# About us section content

We are an independent project ran by the Human Singularity Non-Profit Organization. 

Our mission is to build a comprehensive knowledge hub that tracks research and clinical trials for reversing damage caused by multiple sclerosis, Alzheimer's, Parkinson's, and other neuro-degenerative diseases.

As part of our mission, we apply AI models to filter the most promissing research.

Our work is possible thanks to research groups and individuals who curate each research area with their guidance and expertise.

## Current Curators

### REGENERAR
https://regenerar.eu/

The REGENERAR research project "Improving the Effectiveness and Safety of Epigenetic Editing in Brain Regeneration", led by researcher Lino Ferreira from the Faculty of Medicine of the University of Coimbra, the Center for Neuroscience and Cell Biology of the University of Coimbra (CNC-UC), and the associated laboratory Center for Innovative Biomedicine and Biotechnology (CIBB), aims to use epigenetic tools that allow certain brain cells to be reprogrammed into neurons. Essentially, it involves using what the human body already has, manipulating cells that have increased in number after the disease process into neuronal cells that are very important for brain function.

#### Led by Prof. Lino Ferreira
Researcher at Center of Neurosciences and Cell Biology and Biocant 
https://www.linkedin.com/in/linoferreira/


### LPJ Lab, Neuroimmunometabolism
LPJ Lab, led by Prof. Luca Peruzzotti-Jametti, is dedicated to investigating how cell metabolism shapes brain-immune interactions to pioneer new therapies for MS and other Central Nervous System disorders.

#### Led by Prof. Luca Peruzzotti-Jametti


### Institute for CNS, Blood and Peripheral Inflammation
Focuses on the role of inflammation in the emergence or progression of neurodevelopmental and neurodegenerative disorders.

#### Led by Prof. Adelaide Fernandes
Associate Professor Faculdade de Farmácia da Universidade de Lisboa, Principal Investigator at IMED - Research Institute for Medicines.
